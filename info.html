The Real-Time Paradigm Shift

Understanding the transition from the 2025 Retrospective Challenge to the 2026 Real-Time Edition is critical. The rules have fundamentally changed to mimic realistic monitoring scenarios where look-ahead is impossible.

25

2025 Retrospective

  • ✕
    Batch Delivery: Full segments provided at once, allowing for distribution matching of the entire future window.
  • ✕
    Known Boundary: The break was always at a known point; you just had to verify if it happened.
  • ✕
    Single Score: One prediction per series, prioritizing accuracy over latency.
26

2026 Streaming

  • ✓
    Sequential Streaming: Data arrives one point at a time. No look-ahead allowed.
  • ✓
    Unknown Location: Break can occur anywhere in the online segment or not at all (p=0.5).
  • ✓
    Stepwise Scoring: Confidence score required at every single step, penalizing late detection.

TS-AUC: Scoring the "Step"

The Time-Stratified AUC measures how well your model discriminates between "already broken" series and "stable" series at every time step. To win, your model must output a perfect step function.

Ideal Score: 0 until the break, then instant 1.
Detection Score: The area between lines is your error margin.
Pro Tip: Raw statistical p-values fail because they aren't globally calibrated. Your scores must rank series cross-sectionally.

Developer Lab: The API Contract

Strict adherence to the `yield` pattern is required for deterministic execution.

Memory Limit: Critical 
# The core inference loop
def infer(datasets, model_path):
    model = load(model_path)
    
    # Mark generator as ready
    yield

    for hist, online in datasets:
        model.reset_state()
        model.calibrate(hist)
        
        for point in online:
            score = model.predict(point)
            
            # Yield 0 to 1 confidence
            yield score
